General Info


Condoms

Condoms are the only method of birth control that helps prevent the spread of sexually transmitted infections like HIV. On average, condoms can cost up to $2 and are 85% effective. You do not need a prescription to buy condoms and are easily accessible in many different stores and pharmacies.


Effectiveness: 85% effective

Cost: Up to $2

Prescription: Not required

Prevention: Prevents pregnancies and STIs

Duration: Put on before sex


The Birth Control Pill

The birth control pill is a pill that you take everyday in order to prevent a pregnancy. The pill has hormones that stop the sperm from meeting an egg. The pill stops ovulation which means that there is no egg released to fertilize. The pill does not prevent STIs but it does prevent pregnancies. This method of birth control is 91% effective and the cost can range from $0 to $50 and a prescription is required in order to take this medicine.


Effectiveness: 91% effective

Cost: $0-50

Prescription: Prescription is required

Prevention: Prevents pregnancies but does not prevent STIs

Duration: Take once a day


The Birth Control Shot

The birth control shot is an injection you can receive by a medical professional every 3 months. This injection contains hormone progestin that prevents pregnancy by stopping ovulation. This shot does not prevent STIs, however it is 94% effective in preventing unwanted pregnancies. The injection can cost from $0 to $100.


Effectiveness: 94% effective

Cost:$0-100

Prescription: Injected by doctor or nurse

Duration: One shot every 3 months


The Birth Control Patch

The birth control patch is a transdermal contraceptive patch that you wear on the skin of your belly, upper arm, butt, or back. The patch releases hormones that prevent the sperm from meeting an egg to stop unwanted pregnancies. Although the patch prevents pregnancies, it will not prevent one from receiving a sexually transmitted infection. The patch is 91% effective and can cost up to $150, however you may be able to get it for free from certain organizations. This type of birth control does require a prescription.


Effectiveness: 91% effective

Cost: $0-150

Prescription: Required

Prevention: Prevents pregnancies but does not prevent STIs

Duration: Change patch every week for 3 weeks
